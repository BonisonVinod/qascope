# QAScope Sales Strategy: Parkinson's Law Edition (AI-Scaled)

> **Parkinson's Law:** "Work expands to fill the time available for its completion."
>
> **The flip:** If you give yourself 60 minutes to close a pipeline task, you'll do in 60 minutes what would otherwise take all week. Compress the deadline, compress the work.

> **The AI multiplier:** Use AI tools (NotebookLM, Gemini, Claude, Pomelli) to do the grunt work — research, personalization, copywriting — so your 25-minute block produces 5x the output.

---

## Key Terms (Plain English)

| Term | What It Actually Means | Example |
|------|----------------------|---------|
| **Named Prospect** | A real person with a name, job title, company, and contact info. Not a category — a person you can actually email. | *"Rahul Sharma, Director of Ops, ConnectCall BPO, 60 agents, Mumbai, rahul@connectcall.in"* |
| **Discovery Call** | Your first 30-min call with a prospect where you **listen 80%, talk 20%.** You're diagnosing their QA pain, not pitching. At the end you offer to demo QAScope on their own data. | *"How many calls get QA-reviewed today?" → "About 5%." → "Want to see what 100% looks like?"* |
| **Pipeline** | Your list of active prospects at various stages (contacted → replied → call booked → demo done → pilot → paid). It's just a Google Sheet. | A spreadsheet with 30 rows and a "Stage" column. |
| **Cold Email** | An email to someone who doesn't know you exist. Short (3–4 sentences), personalized, with one ask: "Open to 15 minutes?" | Not a sales pitch. Not a product brochure. Just a short human message. |
| **Bump Email** | A 1-line follow-up to someone who didn't reply. Sent 4 days after the first email. | *"Hey Rahul — did this land in the right inbox?"* |
| **Pilot** | A free or low-cost trial period where the prospect uses QAScope on real data for 2–4 weeks before deciding to pay. | Upload 50 of their conversations, score them, show the dashboard. |

---

## Rules

1. Every task has a **hard time-box.** When the timer rings, you stop and move on.
2. No task takes longer than **25 minutes.** If it does, you scoped it wrong.
3. **Done > Perfect.** A sent email beats a drafted email. A booked call beats a researched prospect.
4. **Let AI do the research and writing. You do the sending and talking.**
5. Review progress once a week (Friday, 15 min). No mid-week second-guessing.

## Week 1: "Build the List" (5 days, 45 min/day)

**Goal: 50 named prospects in a Google Sheet.**

| Day | Task | Time-Box | AI Assists You | Done When |
|-----|------|----------|----------------|-----------|
| **Mon** | Open Apollo.io. Apply filter: India + BPO/Call Center + 20–500 employees + Founder/CEO/Director Ops/Head of QA. Export first 15 contacts. | 20 min | Apollo does the filtering | 15 rows in sheet |
| **Tue** | Export 15 more. Switch city filter (Mumbai → Bangalore → Hyderabad → Pune). | 15 min | Apollo | 30 rows |
| **Wed** | Open LinkedIn. Search your 1st-degree connections for BPO contacts. Add 10 warm contacts with "Source: Warm" tag. | 20 min | LinkedIn search | 40 rows |
| **Thu** | Export 10 more from Apollo (try "Process Excellence" or "Quality Manager" titles). Fill LinkedIn URL column for all 50. | 20 min | Apollo | 50 rows with LinkedIn URLs |
| **Fri** | **AI research sprint:** Copy all 50 names + companies into NotebookLM or Gemini. Ask: *"For each company, write one sentence about what BPO campaign they likely run based on their website."* Star the top 20 most promising. | 25 min | **NotebookLM / Gemini does the company research** | 20 starred rows with "why" notes |

**Week 1 output:** 50 named prospects. 20 starred. ~100 minutes total.

## Friday 15-Min Review (Weekly)

Every Friday at 5 PM, answer these 6 questions in your Google Sheet:

1. **How many emails did I send this week?** (Target: 20)
2. **How many replies did I get?** (Target: 2–3)
3. **How many calls are on next week's calendar?** (Target: 1–2)
4. **How many active pilots right now?** (Target: 1–3)
5. **What's the #1 reason prospects aren't replying?** (Fix it next week)
6. **Am I spending more than 30 min/day on sales?** (If yes, let AI do more of the writing)

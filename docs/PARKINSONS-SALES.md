# QAScope Sales Strategy: Parkinson's Law Edition

> **Parkinson's Law:** "Work expands to fill the time available for its completion."
>
> **The flip:** If you give yourself 60 minutes to close a pipeline task, you'll do in 60 minutes what would otherwise take all week. Compress the deadline, compress the work.

**Rules:**
1. Every task has a **hard time-box.** When the timer rings, you stop and move on.
2. No task takes longer than **25 minutes.** If it does, you scoped it wrong.
3. **Done > Perfect.** A sent email beats a drafted email. A booked call beats a researched prospect.
4. Review progress once a week (Friday, 15 min). No mid-week second-guessing.

---

## Week 1: "Build the List" (5 days, 45 min/day)

The only goal this week: **have 30 named prospects in a Google Sheet.**

| Day | Task | Time-Box | Done When |
|-----|------|----------|-----------|
| **Mon** | Open Apollo.io. Apply filter: India + BPO + 20–200 employees + Founder/CEO/Director Ops. Export first 10 contacts to Google Sheet. | 20 min | 10 rows in sheet |
| **Tue** | Export 10 more contacts from Apollo. Different city filter (Mumbai → Bangalore). | 15 min | 20 rows in sheet |
| **Wed** | Open LinkedIn. Search your 1st-degree connections for anyone at a BPO. Add 5 warm contacts to sheet with "Source: Warm" tag. | 20 min | 25 rows in sheet |
| **Thu** | Export 5 more from Apollo. Fill in LinkedIn URL column for all 30 rows. | 20 min | 30 rows, all with LinkedIn URLs |
| **Fri** | Review the 30 names. Star the top 10 you'd most want as a customer. Write one sentence per starred name: why they'd care. | 25 min | 10 starred rows with "why" notes |

**Week 1 output:** A Google Sheet with 30 prospects. 10 starred. Total time invested: ~100 minutes.

## Week 2: "Send 10 Emails" (5 days, 30 min/day)

The only goal this week: **10 personalized cold emails sent.** Not drafted. Sent.

| Day | Task | Time-Box | Done When |
|-----|------|----------|-----------|
| **Mon** | Pick starred prospect #1 and #2. Open their LinkedIn profiles. Spend 2 min each finding one specific signal (a post, a job change, a campaign mention). Write and **send** both emails using the cold email prompt from `OUTBOUND.md`. | 25 min | 2 emails sent |
| **Tue** | Prospect #3 and #4. Same drill: 2 min LinkedIn scan → write → send. | 25 min | 4 emails sent |
| **Wed** | Prospect #5 and #6. | 25 min | 6 emails sent |
| **Thu** | Prospect #7 and #8. | 25 min | 8 emails sent |
| **Fri** | Prospect #9 and #10. Then check inbox for any replies from Mon/Tue. Reply within 5 min if positive. | 30 min | 10 emails sent. Replies handled. |

**Week 2 output:** 10 hand-personalized emails in the world. Total time invested: ~130 minutes.

---

## Week 3: "Book 2 Calls" (5 days, 25 min/day)

The only goal this week: **2 discovery calls scheduled on your calendar.**

| Day | Task | Time-Box | Done When |
|-----|------|----------|-----------|
| **Mon** | Send Day 4 bump email to prospects #1–#4 who haven't replied. One line: *"Did this land in the right inbox?"* | 10 min | 4 bumps sent |
| **Tue** | Send bump to prospects #5–#8. Check replies from last week — if anyone said "tell me more," reply with a Calendly link. | 15 min | Bumps sent. Calendly links shared. |
| **Wed** | Pick 5 new prospects from your sheet (non-starred). Send fresh cold emails. | 25 min | 5 new emails sent |
| **Thu** | Send Day 4 bumps to prospect #9–#10. Send a **WhatsApp message** to any warm contacts who haven't replied to email (use the WhatsApp pitch from `PITCH.md`). | 20 min | WhatsApp messages sent |
| **Fri** | Review inbox. Confirm any booked calls. If zero calls booked, pick your 3 best non-responders and **call them directly on the phone.** 2-minute pitch each. | 25 min | ≥2 calls on calendar (or 3 phone attempts logged) |

**Week 3 output:** 2 discovery calls booked. Total time invested: ~95 minutes.

## Week 4: "Run 2 Demos & Close 1 Pilot" (5 days, 40 min/day)

The only goal this week: **1 pilot customer actively using QAScope.**

| Day | Task | Time-Box | Done When |
|-----|------|----------|-----------|
| **Mon** | Prep for Discovery Call #1. Re-read their LinkedIn. Write down 3 questions to ask about their QA process. | 15 min | 3 questions written down |
| **Tue** | Run Discovery Call #1. Follow the script from `SALES.md`. Listen 80%, talk 20%. At the end, offer to set up their workspace and score 50 conversations free. | 30 min | Call done. Next step agreed. |
| **Wed** | Set up their QAScope workspace. Upload their sample CSV (or use `test-data/sample-conversations.csv` if they haven't sent data yet). Score a batch. Screenshot the dashboard. Send the screenshots over WhatsApp with: *"Here's what your QA dashboard looks like on day 1."* | 25 min | Workspace live. Screenshots sent. |
| **Thu** | Run Discovery Call #2 (same playbook as Tuesday). | 30 min | Call done. Next step agreed. |
| **Fri** | Follow up with Call #1 prospect: *"Did you get a chance to look at the dashboard? Anything surprising?"* If they say yes, ask: *"Want to move to the paid Starter plan so we lock in this workspace permanently?"* | 15 min | 1 pilot customer active OR clear next-step set for next week |

**Week 4 output:** 1 active pilot customer. Total time invested: ~115 minutes.

## The Daily Micro-Routine (After Week 4, Ongoing)

Once you have momentum, compress your entire daily sales work into this **25-minute block** every weekday morning:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  THE 25-MINUTE SALES BLOCK (10:30 AM daily)     │
│                                                  │
│  Minutes 0–5:   Check inbox. Reply to warm leads │
│  Minutes 5–15:  Send 2 new personalized emails   │
│  Minutes 15–20: Send 2 follow-up bumps           │
│  Minutes 20–25: Post 1 LinkedIn comment on a     │
│                 BPO operator's post (not yours)   │
│                                                  │
│  STOP. Close email. Go build product.            │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Monthly output from this routine:**
- 44 new outbound emails sent (2/day × 22 working days)
- 44 follow-up bumps sent
- 22 LinkedIn engagement touches
- Expected: 3–4 replies → 1–2 calls → 1 new pilot per month

## Friday 15-Min Review (Weekly)

Every Friday at 5 PM, answer these 5 questions in your Google Sheet:

1. **How many emails did I send this week?** (Target: 10)
2. **How many replies did I get?** (Target: 1–2)
3. **How many calls are on next week's calendar?** (Target: 1)
4. **What's the #1 reason prospects aren't replying?** (Fix it next week)
5. **Am I spending more than 30 min/day on sales?** (If yes, you're over-engineering it)

---

## Why This Works (The Parkinson's Insight)

Most solo founders treat sales as an open-ended, anxiety-inducing chore. So they either:
- **Spend 4 hours "researching" prospects** instead of emailing them (procrastination disguised as preparation)
- **Spend 0 hours** because the task feels too big to start

Parkinson's Law says: **compress the container, compress the work.**

- A 25-minute time-box forces you to send the email *now*, not polish it for an hour.
- A "2 emails per day" quota is so small it feels ridiculous to skip.
- A weekly review in 15 minutes prevents you from building elaborate tracking systems you'll never maintain.

**The math is simple:** 10 emails/week × 6% reply rate × 30% pilot conversion = ~1 new pilot every 5 weeks. That's ₹1.16L MRR added per pilot (80-agent campaign). Do this for 6 months and you have ₹6–7L MRR from cold outbound alone.

The bottleneck was never your product. It's always been: *did you send the email today?*
